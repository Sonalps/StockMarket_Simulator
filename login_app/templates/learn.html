STOCK MARKET



A stock represents a share in the ownership of a company and constitutes a claim on part of the company's assets and earnings.
While the stock market is a collection of markets and exchanges where the buying, selling, and issuance of shares of publicly held companies take place.

How the Stock Market Works
Initial Public Offering (IPO): When a company decides to go public, it issues shares through an IPO. This allows the company to raise capital from public investors.
Trading: Investors can buy and sell stocks through exchanges or OTC markets. This is facilitated by brokers who act as intermediaries.
Prices: Stock prices fluctuate based on supply and demand, influenced by factors like company performance, economic data, and market sentiment.
Indexes: Stock market indexes, like the S&P 500 or the Dow Jones Industrial Average, track the performance of a group of stocks to give an overall view of the market's health.

Importance of the Stock Market:
Capital Formation: Companies raise funds for expansion, innovation, and growth.
Wealth Creation: Provides opportunities for investors to grow their wealth through capital gains and dividends.
Economic Indicator: Reflects the economic health and investor sentiment.
Participants in the Stock Market.
Individual Investors: Retail investors who buy and sell stocks for personal gain.
Institutional Investors: Entities like mutual funds, pension funds, and insurance companies that trade large volumes of stocks.
Brokers and Dealers: Intermediaries who facilitate the buying and selling of stocks.
Market Makers: Firms that ensure liquidity by buying and selling stocks from their own accounts.


Key Concepts and Strategies
Bull and Bear Markets: A bull market is characterized by rising stock prices, while a bear market features declining prices.
Diversification: Spreading investments across various assets to reduce risk.
Portfolio: A collection of investments held by an individual or institution.
Dividends: Payments made by a company to its shareholders, usually from profits.
Earnings Per Share (EPS): A company's profit divided by the outstanding shares of its common stock.
Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio: A valuation metric comparing the current share price to its per-share earnings.
